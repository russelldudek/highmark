Candidate vision for Artificial Intelligence (AI) Consultant
by Russell Dudek

AI opportunity is abundant.
Operating value is selective.

Health Plan Operations does not need a longer list of possible use cases. It needs a disciplined way to decide where AI belongs in the work, what evidence must exist, what people retain authority, who owns adoption, and how value becomes measurable.

      02

      The role makes the portfolio decidable.

      The useful question is not “Can AI do something here?” It is “What should advance, under what authority, with which evidence, and who owns the operating result?”

      Advance

      Controlled proof

      A consequential workflow problem, usable evidence, explicit authority, a real owner, and measurable learning are present.

      Assist

      Evidence develops

      Bound AI to preparation, explanation, or routing while provenance, quality, and operating feedback mature.

      Hold

      Readiness first

      Resolve source authority, data quality, integration, policy, stewardship, or ownership before expanding automation.

      Human-led

      Support bounded

      Use AI to improve context and consistency while keeping consequential judgment and relationship ownership with people.

      Stop

      Not appropriate

      Do not advance when the intervention conflicts with human authority, trust, policy, risk, or a defensible value case.

      05

      The first 90 days: earn the right to prioritize.

      The first deliverable is not a grand roadmap. It is a trusted, evidence-backed way to make the next set of decisions better.

      Days 1–30

      Read the work

      Map current initiatives, workflows, systems, data, policy constraints, human authority, ownership, frontline friction, and existing measures. Listen before prescribing.

      • Workflow and stakeholder maps
      • Current portfolio inventory
      • Evidence and authority gaps
      Days 31–60

      Charter the decisions

      Establish a common opportunity record and review cadence so business, technical, governance, adoption, and value partners see the same decision.

      • Opportunity charters
      • Proof and stop criteria
      • Adoption and measurement plans
      Days 61–90

      Run controlled proof

      Advance one or two bounded opportunities, instrument the workflow, capture user feedback, surface exceptions, and return learning to the portfolio.

      • Controlled proof briefs
      • Operating telemetry
      • Scale, hold, or stop review
      Review the complete 90-day entry plan

      06

      Questions I would use to discover the real mandate.

      1. 01

        Where is HPO experiencing the greatest gap between AI interest and implementation capacity?

      2. 02

        Which existing use cases have strong executive support but still struggle with workflow ownership or adoption?

      3. 03

        At what point do Responsible AI, privacy, legal, and security partners enter the opportunity process today?

      4. 04

        Who owns value realization after a proof moves from the AI team into an operating function?

      5. 05

        Which HPO workflows generate repeat friction across members, providers, and internal teams?

      6. 06

        What would make the first six months unmistakably valuable to the Director of AI and HPO leaders?
